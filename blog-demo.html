Student Needs Decoded: How We’re Raising the Bar on Campus Convenience

  • HOPE Team
  • Jun 20, 2025
  • 8 Comments

What if we told you that one of the biggest stressors for students isn’t exams or deadlines — but simply getting everyday essentials on time? That’s right. In our recent survey with over 75 participants, we uncovered some powerful insights into what students struggle with daily when it comes to product accessibility.

The results were eye-opening. From missing out on crucial academic supplies to facing stockouts at inconvenient times, students are clearly looking for a smarter, faster, and more reliable way to get what they need — without breaking their schedule or budget.

“No time to go out” was mentioned by over 70% of students as a major barrier. This insight alone tells us that convenience and timing are non-negotiables.

When it comes to product needs, custom printing and binding services stood out as the most in-demand — followed closely by stationery, basic electronics, and personal hygiene products. These aren’t just wants; they're essential tools for academic success and personal comfort.

And here’s where it gets even more interesting: pricing plays a pivotal role. Over 70% of respondents said they’re willing to spend between ₹20–₹100 per order. Transparency around emergency delivery fees also emerged as a key concern — many students stressed that extra charges should be justified based on the product and urgency.

Timing is Everything — Literally

Students prefer flexibility — but there’s a clear favorite: the noon slot (12:40 PM – 1:15 PM) is golden. Whether it's during a lunch break or between classes, this window offers the perfect opportunity for quick deliveries without disrupting the day.

Missed Opportunities = Room for Growth

An overwhelming 75% admitted to skipping purchases because items weren’t available when they needed them. That’s not just a statistic — it’s a signal that we can do better. By improving stock availability and offering extended hours, including night-time options, we can turn missed chances into successful deliveries.

Speaking of delivery — direct-to-classroom service is the most preferred method, especially among students who value speed and minimal effort. Pick-up points are also popular, proving that a hybrid model could offer the best of both worlds.

What Would Make Life Easier?

When asked what new features would make a real difference, students gave us a clear roadmap:

  • Faster delivery times
  • Affordable and transparent pricing
  • Better stock management
  • More variety — especially hygiene kits and medicine/grocery delivery
  • Improved feedback and support systems

Based on this feedback, we’re already exploring ways to expand our offerings — including hygiene packs, student-friendly bundles, and even limited food delivery. Our goal? To become the go-to solution for every campus need — quickly, reliably, and affordably.

While awareness of HOPE services remains moderate, usage among surveyed students is still relatively low. That means we have an exciting opportunity ahead — to build trust, improve usability, and deliver experiences that match the expectations of today’s students.
